The Almohad Catastrophe (1146–1269) — The Great Conversion

The single most important event for understanding potential Jewish roots in Muslim North African families is the Almohad forced conversion. Mass forced conversions swept Oran (1142), Tlemcen (1145), Fez (1146), and all of Tunisia. Converts were forced to wear distinctive clothing identifying them as former Jews, forbidden from marrying "old Muslims." Thousands converted publicly while practicing Judaism in secret — the anusim.
